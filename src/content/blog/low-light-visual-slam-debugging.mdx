## Context

During my SLAM experiments, I observed that one of the major factors affecting trajectory quality was not only the algorithm pipeline itself, but also the visual quality of the incoming data.

## What went wrong

Trajectory behavior became inconsistent under poorer lighting conditions. This made the system harder to evaluate fairly because instability was appearing before deeper comparison even started.

## How I narrowed it down

I reduced the problem gradually by checking whether the issue came from:
- transforms
- synchronization
- topic structure
- image quality

The strongest clue came from how behavior changed under different visual conditions.

## Takeaway

This reminded me that robotics debugging is rarely about only one algorithm. Often, reliability depends on the entire sensing and system chain.
